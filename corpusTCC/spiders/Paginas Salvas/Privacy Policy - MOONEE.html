1. What kind of Data We Collect?
We do not collect or use information that identifies you personally (i.e., name, address, e-mail, phone number, etc.). However, we do collect and process certain identifiers of our users as detailed below. Our business partners may also provide us with their personal contact details through our website for business use. We collect and process several different types of information about you, as follows:
1.1. Usage Data We are likely to collect and process information that your mobile device or browser sends whenever you visit or use our Services ("Usage Data"). Usage Data may include, but is not limited to:
• Your computer's Internet Protocol address (IP address), browser type, browser version, the pages of our Services that you visit, the time and date of your visit, the time spent on those pages, unique device identifiers and other diagnostic data.
• Your cookie information, you country, regional and language settings, time zone setting, the type of mobile device you use, your mobile device unique ID, the IP address of your mobile device, your mobile operating system, mobile carrier, the screen size or processor of your device, the type of mobile internet browser you use, browser plug-in types and versions, page response times and download errors.
• Gameplay Data – Data pertaining to your activities on our applications and notably the way in which you interact with them (for instance, how and when you use the applications, session time, in-app purchases, install timestamp, etc.) and with the advertisements we display (for instance, time spent on them, potential clicks, etc.). We never receive your payment or credit card information.
• Advertising ID – Apple Identifier for Advertisers on Apple and Google Advertising Identity, or other unique identifiers that advertisers use for personalized advertising. They are consistent across all mobile applications and thus allow cross-app tracking.
• Data relating to the location of your device. Usage Data does not identify you personally, it simply enables us to compile statistics about our users and their use of the Services.
1.2. Tracking and Cookies Data We are also likely to use cookies and similar tracking technologies to track the activity on our Services and hold certain information. Cookies are files with small amount of data which may include an anonymous unique identifier. Cookies are sent to your browser from a website and stored on your device. Tracking technologies also used are beacons, tags, and scripts to collect and track information and to improve and analyze our Services. By using our Services, you agree to us placing these sorts of cookies on your device and accessing them when you visit the Services in future. You can instruct your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. However, if you do not accept cookies, you may not be able to use some portions of our Services.
