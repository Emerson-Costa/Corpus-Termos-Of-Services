Information Collection and Use

Both the Earthquake Network app and the Earthquake Network Pro app send data to the same server.

For the real time detection of earthquakes, the apps periodically send the smartphone coordinates to the app server. This happens every around two hours and when the smartphone detects an acceleration possibly related to an earthquake.

When you use the app to report an earthquake you felt, the smartphone coordinates are sent to the server.

Location data collected by the apps may be used for scientific publications also not directly related to earthquakes, and they are not transferred to third parties.

You may choose to alert a list of trusted contacts by e-mail when an earthquake is detected in real time. The e-mail will include the smartphone coordinates at the moment of the earthquake for search and rescue operations. Your e-mail address and the e-mail addresses of your trusted contacts are saved on the app and never sent to the app server. The e-mail will be sent through your GMail account and the text of the e-mail is never sent to the app server.

You may choose to take part to the chatrooms of the app by creating a personal profile. The profile is linked to either your Google account or your Facebook account. Data about your Google or Facebook account are never collected and sent to the app server. The personal profile is public and all the information are visible to the other users. Chat messages sent on the public chatrooms are sent to the app server and stored for a maximum of 48 hours. Private maessages to other users are delivered to the recipient without being saved on the app server.

The apps do use third party services that may collect information used to identify you.
